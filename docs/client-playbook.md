# Client Playbook — Getting to 3 Clients Without "Marketing"

The core insight: prep center clients aren't won with ads. Sellers go *looking* for prep centers when they need one. Your whole job is to be findable in the places they look, and have the most professional-looking operation when they find you. The portal does the persuading.

Target: 3 clients in 90 days. That's roughly $3–6k/month at typical volumes.

---

## Step 0 — Positioning (one sentence, use it everywhere)

> "East Coast FBA prep in Lansdale, PA — 1-day inbound to the PA/NJ Amazon fulfillment cluster, every unit photographed, live client portal."

Why this works: Pennsylvania sits inside one of the densest Amazon fulfillment regions in the country. Wholesale sellers care about check-in speed and cheap freight to FCs more than they care about sales tax (their resale certificate handles that). You're not "a kid with a warehouse" — you're "East Coast speed with software."

Who you want: **wholesale and private-label sellers** doing 500–3,000 units/month. 
Who to politely decline at first: oversize/heavy freight, hazmat, meltables, clothing with 20 size variants. High effort, low margin, easy to screw up while learning.

## Step 1 — Be findable (week 1, one-time setup)

1. **Google Business Profile.** Create a free listing: "FBA Prep Center — Lansdale, PA." Category: warehouse/fulfillment service. This is the highest-leverage move on this list: sellers literally Google "prep center near me" or "prep center Pennsylvania," and almost no competitors bother with local SEO. Add photos of your racking and label station.
2. **One-page website** on your domain (you're a vibe coder — this is an afternoon): the positioning line, your per-unit pricing table, 3 photos, a 90-second portal demo video, and a contact form/email. Pricing ON the site — sellers skip prep centers that hide pricing.
3. **Get listed in prep center directories.** Search "FBA prep center directory" and submit to every active one you find (most are free). Sellers browse these by state.
4. **Record a Loom walkthrough of the portal** (2 minutes, your voice, click through a demo client). This video is your salesperson. Pin it everywhere.

## Step 2 — Show up where sellers ask (ongoing, ~30 min/day)

Sellers constantly post "anyone know a good prep center?" in:

- **Reddit:** r/FulfillmentByAmazon and r/AmazonSeller — search "prep center" and you'll see the request pattern. Answer helpfully, mention you run one in PA, link the site.
- **Facebook groups:** search "Amazon FBA wholesale," "Amazon seller," and "FBA prep" groups — join the big ones, turn on notifications for "prep center" keyword mentions.
- **Discord/Telegram seller communities:** wholesale and arbitrage groups always have a #services or #prep-centers channel.

Rules for not feeling like a marketer (because you aren't being one):
- Never cold-post ads. Only reply when someone *asks* for a prep center or has a prep problem.
- Lead with a useful answer, mention your operation second.
- One template reply you can reuse: "I run a prep center in Lansdale PA — 1-day inbound to the PA/NJ FC cluster. $X.XX/unit inspect+label, every carton photographed, and you get a live portal showing your counts (demo: [link]). Happy to do your first 100 units free so you can test us."

## Step 3 — The closer offer

**First 100 units free** (or first month at half rate). Costs you maybe $100 of labor; removes all risk for a seller trying an unknown 19-year-old. Once their inventory is physically in your building and they're watching it in the portal, switching costs keep them — prep clients rarely leave a center that's accurate and fast.

During the trial, over-communicate: photo of their boxes the moment they arrive, same-day check-in, a "here's your portal login" email within an hour of receiving. That experience IS your marketing.

## Step 4 — Referrals (how client 1 becomes clients 2–4)

Sellers all know other sellers — they're in masterminds and group chats together. After a smooth first month, one ask: "Know anyone else who needs prep? I'll credit you 200 free units per referral." A referral from inside a seller community is worth more than anything you could ever post yourself.

## Step 5 — Local angle most prep centers ignore

Philly-metro sellers exist and want to *visit* their prep center — being driveable is a trust feature no Montana competitor can match. Mention "tours welcome" on the site. Also: local 3PL overflow — small ecom brands (Shopify) need pick-and-pack too, and your portal works just as well for them.

## The 90-day cadence

- **Weeks 1–2:** GBP listing, one-pager site live, directory submissions, Loom demo recorded. Insurance + LLC done in parallel.
- **Weeks 3–6:** daily 30-min community monitoring, answer every prep request you see. Goal: 5 conversations, 1 trial client.
- **Weeks 7–12:** nail the first client's experience, ask for the referral, land #2–3. Raise nothing, change nothing, just be fast and accurate.

## What a "conversation → client" looks like

1. Seller DMs after seeing your reply → 
2. You send the Loom + pricing sheet → 
3. 15-min call (they mostly want to confirm you're a real human with a real building — show the warehouse on video) → 
4. They send a test shipment under the free-100 offer → 
5. You check it in same-day with photos in the portal → 
6. They send the rest of their volume.

Failure mode to avoid: don't get stuck polishing the website/portal for weeks instead of replying to seller posts. Findability beats polish. Ship the ugly one-pager; reply to threads today.
