# Simple Service Agreement Outline — Not Legal Advice

Use this as a checklist for an attorney-reviewed one-pager before taking inventory.

## Parties

- Prep center legal name
- Client legal name
- Effective date

## Services

The prep center may receive, inspect, photograph, label, polybag, bundle, store, and forward ecommerce inventory based on written client instructions.

## Client responsibilities

Client is responsible for:

- Accurate SKU data and prep instructions
- Supplier tracking numbers
- Amazon/FBA shipment plans and requirements
- Product compliance, restricted product rules, and authenticity
- Timely payment

## Fees

Attach the pricing sheet:

- Receiving/inspection
- Labeling
- Polybagging
- Bundling/custom prep
- Storage
- Returns/disposal
- Special projects

## Payment terms

- Invoices due on receipt or within X days
- Work may pause if invoice is overdue
- Storage fees continue on unpaid inventory

## Liability cap

Set a clear liability cap. Example structure to have reviewed:

- Prep center is not liable for supplier defects, Amazon decisions, carrier delays, or marketplace account issues.
- Prep center liability for lost/damaged goods is capped at the lesser of actual wholesale cost or $X per unit, unless caused by gross negligence or intentional misconduct.

## Inventory rules

- Client retains title to inventory
- Inventory may be quarantined if damaged, unidentified, unpaid, unsafe, or non-compliant
- Prep center can refuse hazmat, meltables, perishables, regulated goods, or oversized freight

## Insurance

Client understands prep center maintains applicable business coverage. Do not use this section until coverage is actually active.

## Termination

Either party can terminate with written notice. Client must pay open balances and arrange removal/shipment of remaining goods.

## Signatures

Prep center signature/date
Client signature/date
